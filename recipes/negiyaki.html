Negiyaki

What is negiyaki?

Negiyaki is a Japanese savory pancake made with a thin batter and lots of chopped green onions.

Ingredients

  • 14 green onions/scallions
  • 4 oz sliced pork belly
  • ½ cup katsuobushi
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 4 Tbsp tenkasu/agedama
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ tsp soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup water
  • neutral oil (for frying)
  • toppings of your choice (brush with soy sauce or okonomi sauce, drizzle with Japanese mayonnaise, and/or sprinkle with more katsuobushi)

Steps

  1. Thinly slice green onions.
  2. Cut pork belly slices into 5-inch strips.
  3. Grind katsuobushi into a powder.
  4. In a large bowl, combine sliced green onions and all-purpose flour; toss to coat.
  5. Add baking powder, tenkasu, katsuobushi powder, eggs, soy sauce, and water. Mix until just combined.
  6. Divide the batter into two equal portions.
  7. Heat two frying pans over medium heat and add neutral oil to each.
  8. Pour half the batter into each pan, spreading into a round shape about 5-6 inches across.
  9. Sprinkle katsuobushi powder on top and lay pork belly slices over each pancake. Cook uncovered for 2 minutes.
  10. Flip the pancakes and reduce heat to low. Cover and cook for 4 minutes.
  11. Uncover and flip again. Increase heat to medium, and cook uncovered for 1 minute.
  12. Transfer pancakes to plates with the pork belly side up. Serve with desired toppings.
