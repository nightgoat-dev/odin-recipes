Chicken Katsu

What is chicken katsu?

Chicken katsu is a Japanese dish consisting of a breaded and deep-fried chicken cutlet. The chicken is coated in flour, egg, and panko breadcrumbs before being fried to a crispy, golden brown. It is often served with a tangy tonkatsu sauce and shredded cabbage.

Ingredients

  • 1 piece boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • ½ tsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt
  • ⅛ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 cups neutral oil (for deep-frying)
  • 3 Tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup panko
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ Tbsp neutral oil (for the egg)
  • tonkatsu sauce
  • shredded green cabbage

Steps

  1. Slice boneless, skinless chicken breasts horizontally to create thin cutlets. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Arrange three shallow dishes: one with all-purpose flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with panko breadcrumbs.
  3. Dredge each chicken cutlet in flour, ensuring a light, even coating. Dip into the beaten eggs, allowing excess to drip off. Finally, coat thoroughly with panko, pressing gently to adhere.
  4. In a deep skillet or frying pan, heat neutral oil to 340ºF (170ºC).
  5. Carefully place the breaded cutlets into the hot oil. Fry for a total of 3 minutes, flipping it over at the halfway point.
  6. Transfer the cooked cutlets to a wire rack or paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Slice into strips and serve with tonktasu sauce and shredded cabbage.
